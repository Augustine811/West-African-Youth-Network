Background Information

The West African Youth Network (WAYN) is a youth-led, voluntary organization established in 2001 in Liberia and relocated to Sierra Leone in 2003. Since its inception, WAYN has been committed to empowering young people in Sierra Leone by promoting peacebuilding, governance, human rights, and other social issues affecting Youth and education across the sub-region.

The organization actively supports youth inclusion in governance, strengthens border communities to enhance security, and develops the skills of young people in public speaking and leadership.

WAYN serves as a key catalyst for increasing youth participation in governance, human rights, and other social issues affecting Youth, the development of public policy. Through empowerment, capacity building, and advocacy, it amplifies youth voices in policymaking and public reforms. The organization's vision is to nurture confident, engaged young leaders who contribute to social change, human rights advancement, and effective governance in Sierra Leone.

Emerging after the civil war that lasted from 1991 to 2002, WAYN addresses ongoing challenges facing the majority youth population, including political violence, youth unemployment, police brutality, student unrest, and gender inequality. The network maintains strong partnerships with international NGOs and progressive movements to drive development and positive social transformation.

WAYN's dynamic team consists largely of young leaders under 35, many still in university or involved in apprenticeship programs. Officially registered with the Ministry of Youth, the organization leverages grants and collaborations to implement impactful programs fostering peaceful coexistence and meaningful youth engagement in shaping Sierra Leone's future.
